Biking stuff:

					Okay my classes are on spring break, and my plan was to build up my endurance on my bike 6/7 days a week.
					I'm about 10 days in and I've done maybe 3 days of real practice and now I'm sick and can't go out.
					I did some really fun trips that were longer than I've ever gone though so I'm still happy.
					I'm still really slow, and I don't think my single gear bike is helping, but I wonder how much improvement I'd see switching to a nicer road bike.
				
					This one was the day I bought my new bike. It's just a cheap single speed, but it rides a lot smoother than my old one.
					This route down the river is quite nice until it dumps you onto a really busy road with no sidewalks.
					I found out after that if you cross the river the path continues, I'd like to bike all the way to Osaka and back soon as a challenge to myself.
				
					This one goes to the nicest area to ride near my house, and I decided to do a couple of hikes. (You can click the little images in the corner of the map).
					My legs stopped hurting so bad after 10+km of biking but adding hiking in makes it really tough to bike up the hill back home.
				
					Kamogawa (the big north to south river) has the nicest bike / pedestrian path in Kyoto, but you have to bike on some busy streets to get there from where we live.
					This day I went to the northern part, basically until the nice pavement ended. I went to a nice french bakery and looped around a bit as I've never been to most of the stuff up here.
